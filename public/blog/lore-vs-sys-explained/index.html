LORE vs SYS Mode: How the Archive Sees Anime Differently

Every universe in the archive can be read two ways: as a story (LORE) or as an engineered system (SYS). Here's why that matters and how to use it.

The Anime Architecture Archive doesn't just describe anime — it decodes them. Every universe in the archive supports two reading modes: LORE and SYS.

LORE Mode: The Story Layer

LORE mode is for fans. It describes characters, events, and mechanics in human-readable narrative language. 'Eren becomes the catalyst for the Rumbling' — that's LORE.

SYS Mode: The System Layer

SYS mode treats the anime as an engineered system. Same character, same event, but framed as infrastructure: 'A deterministic execution node triggers a global catastrophic cascade.' SYS mode reveals the structural mechanics behind the narrative.

Why Two Modes?

Because anime worlds ARE systems. They have rules, constraints, failure modes, and emergent behavior. Seeing both layers simultaneously is what makes the archive different from every wiki out there.

Try it yourself — open any universe and toggle the mode switch. Start with Attack on Titan for the most dramatic contrast.
